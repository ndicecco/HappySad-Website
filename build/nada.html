50 Shades of Vinyl

aka

Sex? But I'm Canadian!

The stories never feel dirty; rather, they are funny, heart-warming and sweet with a positive message. It really doesn’t get much more Canadian than this.
Dicecco can do the voice, and gets about as close one could imagine to McLean’s speaking cadence. The writing is spot on — it’s like someone found the lost R-rated recordings of The Vinyl Café and put them onstage.
Dicecco obviously reveres McLean and his legacy. And his impression is uncannily accurate. CBC Radio fans, buy your tickets now...
The show’s main strength is its carefully-crafted narrative that allows for a faultless pacing. The performance is polished and appears effortless—a sign that a lot of care has been put into the show.
Dicecco captures the voice, intonation and rhythm of McLean perfectly.
Never feeling forced, every word and gesture had a purpose in telling the sometimes ridiculously funny, and bawdy, stories.
